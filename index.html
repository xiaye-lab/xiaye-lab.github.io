PhD Researcher | College of Design and Engineering, NUS

I am a PhD student at the National University of Singapore. My research decodes the intersection of urban sustainability, building decarbonization, and spatial analytics.

I leverage data-driven methodologies—including machine learning, large-scale geospatial analysis, and agent-based modeling—to understand how cities evolve and how we can design them for optimal energy performance and environmental equity.

Research Focus

  • Urban Morphology & GNNs: Developing Graph Neural Network models (GAT/GATv2) to analyze spatial spillovers and the trade-offs between Environmental and Societal Performance Indices in Singapore.
  • Decarbonization Strategies: Conducting comparative analyses of building decarbonization policies and strategies between high-density cities like Singapore and Hong Kong.
  • Agent-Based Energy Management: Designing multi-agent systems using the OpenClaw framework to simulate and manage energy consumption and thermal comfort across the NUS campus.
